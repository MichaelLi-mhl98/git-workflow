Social-media impressions and CTRs versus industry norms

– We're averaging roughly 150k-300k Facebook impressions per month on Adzenys (and 100k-180k on Cotempla) in line with our $1k–3k monthly budgets. That volume is healthy for a niche, B2C‐style healthcare brand.

– Benchmark: ~0.8–1.0% link CTR for healthcare/pharma ads

• Cost per link click

– Cotempla FB: $1.95–$28.57 (wide swings when low volume)

Bounce rate – should we worry?
We don't yet have a quoted bounce-rate figure in our decks, but best practice for consumer‐focused healthcare sites is generally a 40–60% bounce rate. Anything consistently above ~60–65% signals that visitors aren't finding what they expect and often warrants a landing-page audit (messaging alignment, load speed, navigation, stronger calls to action, etc.).
Action steps:
• Pull the last 30- and 90-day site bounce rates in GA4 (or your analytics platform) and segment by traffic source (paid social vs. organic search vs. referral).
• If paid-social visitors are bouncing above ~60%, test optimizing your headline-to-headline match and tighten up the patient journey (slide 42, "optimize patient journey on branded websites").
• Continue to monitor weekly, and include bounce rate as a "performance" KPI alongside time on site and conversion rate, per our strategic plan.


05/22/2025 14:46:36 : If I had to spend more money on social media, what would you recommend? How can we improve performance with the budget we have?


Here's how I'd think about allocating incremental social‐media dollars – and how to get more out of the budget you already have:

Double-down on your top-performers
• Adzenys Facebook campaigns deliver 1.3–2.1% link-click CTR at $1.17–$3.19 CPC, versus a 0.8–1.0% industry norm and $2.50–4.00 typical CPC in pharma. That means every extra dollar here drives better engagement and more traffic to your site than most of our other paid-social bets. I'd shift any incremental spend into the Adzenys creative/ad-set combinations hitting ≥1.8% link CTR today.
• Cotempla Facebook is in-line on link CTR (~1.1%) but swings wildly on CPC when volumes are low. Instead of a straight pro-rata lift, put new budget against the stronger months (April–September) and pause the low-volume pushes (e.g. June or October) until we've optimized targeting.
Tighten your targeting and creative
• Layer in look-alike audiences seeded with your highest-value audiences (e.g. physicians or patients who have already converted).
• Split-test new copy, imagery, and video formats vs. our current "static image + carousel" batch. Even a 10–15% lift in link CTR compounds nicely over $100k+ of spend.
• Add sequential retargeting: users who click but don't convert see a follow-up message; those who watch 50%+ of a video move into a "more in-depth" funnel ad.
Optimize the on-site experience
• Our paid-social bounce‐rate threshold should be ≤60%. If FB-driven visitors are bouncing at, say, 70%, run a landing-page A/B test: headline alignment, load-speed improvements, stronger call-to-action, tighter "above-the-fold" content. A 5–10% lift in post-click conversion is equivalent to a 5–10% budget increase with zero extra media spend.
• Hook in the Facebook pixel (or Conversions API) so we can optimize for actual sign-ups or scripts rather than just clicks.
Test adjacent platforms/formats
• Instagram Stories and Reels: often 20–30% lower CPCs than feed ads, with comparable or better swipe-up (link) rates in our category.
• LinkedIn Sponsored Content for HCP targeting (especially if we expand into nurse-practitioner/PA segments), even in a small pilot, can deliver higher‐intent traffic at a premium.
Keep your ROI bar visible
• Social-Media ROI today sits at ~2.4× (per the channel-level ROI table). That's good, but it lags behind Calls (4.3×) and HCP webinars (2.6×). As you deploy incremental dollars, track ROI by tactic weekly.
• If Facebook link-CPC creeps above ~$3.50 or link-CTR falls below ~1.2%, pause scale‐ups and reallocate back into the next-best-ROI lever (e.g. additional call campaigns or expanded paid search).
Bottom line: if you must spend more on social, put it behind the proven Adzenys Facebook sets, invest in creative/targeting tests to drive another 15–20% lift in CTR, shore up landing-page conversion (to capture more of those clicks) and pilot complementary formats (Stories, LinkedIn). That combination will stretch every incremental dollar farther than just "turning the overall social budget up."
